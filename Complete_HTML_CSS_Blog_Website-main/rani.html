Rani Lakshmi Bai - Rani of Jhansi [NCERT Notes: Modern Indian History For UPSC]

Rani Lakshmibai was born on November 19, 1828 in Varanasi city. Her name was Manikarnika Tambe and her nickname was Manu. Her father was Moropant Thambe and her mother was Bhagirathi Sapule (Bhagirathi Bhai) from present-day Maharashtra. Her mother died when she was four years old. Her father was a war commander under Peshwabazi his Rao II of Bitor district. Growing up at home, she was literate and grew up more independent than others her age. Her studies included shooting, horseback riding and fencing, which were at odds with the cultural expectations of women in Indian society at the time. In 1842, at the age of 14, she married the Maharajah of Jhansi, Gangadar Rao. After marriage she was called Lakshmibai. A son Damodar Rao was born to her in 1851. However, he died four months later. Gangadhar Rao died in 1853. Before he died, he adopted his cousin's son Anand his Rao and renamed him Damodar his Rao.

Rani Lakshmi Bai Contributions in Indian Freedom Struggle


Lord Dalhousie (born 22 April 1812) attempted to annex Jhansi using the doctrine of fragility, as the king had no natural heir when the Maharajah died. He was therefore given an annual pension and asked to leave the fortress of Jhansi. 1857 Rebellion broke out in his Meerut and Rani ruled his Jhansi as regent for his minor son. In , British troops under the command of Sir Hugh Rose arrived at Fort Jhansi with the intention of capturing it in 1858. He demanded that the city surrender to him, or it would Destroyed. Rani Laxmibai rejected this and declared: According to Lord Krishna's words, if one is victorious, he will enjoy the fruits of victory, and even if he is defeated and killed on the battlefield, he certainly deserves eternal glory and salvation. Her male and female armies against England. Despite a valiant fight, Jhansi lost the battle. Rani, who had his young son tied to his back, fled to Carpi on horseback. Together with Tatya Tope and other rebel soldiers, the Rani captured Fort Gwalior. She then made her way to Morar in Gwalior to fight the British. Rani Laxmibai She died at the Battle of Gwalior on 18 June 1858 when she was 23 years old. She died as a soldier. Sir Hugh Rose commented: "She is remarkable for her beauty, her wisdom and her endurance, and she is the most dangerous of the rebel leaders.". Rani Laxmibai became for later Indian nationalists a symbol of resistance to British rule. She will forever be remembered as a great martyr who gave her life for freedom. She is a symbol of courage, heroism and girl power.
